<font size="5"><u><b>Bangkok</b></u></font><br />
Bangkok is a big city comprises of many nationalities today, its one of the most popular residential city of foreigners; it is situated in the middle of Thailand and at the mouth of the Chao Phraya river. The city is densely populated, however, which also makes the city more convenient, and things are within the reach of finger tips. Especially for tourism, Bangkok has one of the most developed tourism industry in the world. The city includes both western and eastern restaurants, and has many sight seen destinations.<br />
The transportations are convenient as well, for example, taxis starts at B35 (= $1) and B5 every 100 meter, taxis are all over Bangkok (you can see at least tones of them on any road), as well as sky train (MRT stations) which stretches out all over Bangkok. <a href="http://bangkok.sawadee.com/mrta.htm" target="_blank">http://bangkok.sawadee.com/mrta.htm</a><br />
<br />
<u><b>Famous Thai Restaurants</b></u><br />
<b>Cabbages and Condoms</b><br />
Address: 6 Sukhumvit Soi 12 Bangkok 10110<br />
Telephone: 02-229-4611<br />
<b>Tom Yom Gung</b><br />
Address: Khao San Road Banglampoo Bangkok<br />
<b>Sala Thai</b><br />
Address: 120/126 Rajaprarop Road, Bangkok Indra Regent hotel Bangkok  <br />
Tel: 02 208-0022-33<br />
<b>Foreign Restaurants</b><br />
As well as branched restaurants such as MK, and foreign restaurants Sizzler (Salad bar), Pizza Company, MacDonald's.<br />
<br />
<u><b>Sight seen in Bangkok</b></u><br />
<i>There are many temples and monuments:</i><br />
<b>Wat Phra Kaew &amp; Grand Palace</b><br />
These two desitnations are one of the most tourist attracted sites, Wat Phra Kaew comprises of many small Wats that are the typical traditional Thai Buddhist Temples, Wat Phra Kaew is built in 1782, and is densely decorated, pillars and roofs are well ornate with mirrors and glasses. From far, this inner structure glitters and sparkles. The Grand Palace is right adjacent to Wat Phra and is built during the reign of King Rama V, although not used by the King any more except for ceremonies and Coronation Day. The admission fee is B200 for both.<br />
<img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3250/3013895266_3070020609.jpg" alt="" style="border:0px; max-width: 240px; width: expression(this.width > 240 ? 240: true); max-height: 600px; height: expression(this.height > 600 ? 600: true);" /><br />
<b>Wat Pho</b><br />
Wat in Thai means (temple) and Wat Pho is the biggest temple in Bangkok, and this temple is well decorate and ornate that it is majestic from its outside appearance. Wat Pho is most famous for its biggest reclining Buddha (shown on the right picture). It is very close to the Grand Palace, so you may visit it on the same trip. <br />
<img src="http://farm4.static.flickr.com/3294/3013895262_c54d458dee.jpg" alt="" style="border:0px; max-width: 240px; width: expression(this.width > 240 ? 240: true); max-height: 600px; height: expression(this.height > 600 ? 600: true);" /><br />
<i>Parks &amp; Shopping</i><br />
<b>Lumphini Park</b><br />
Located in the center of Bangkok, lies on the North of Rama IV Road, it has the city&acirc;��s biggest dance hall and public library. Established during the reign of King Rama IV in 1920s, many activities take place in it, such as concerts, morning exercises, riding boats, and food both for sale.<br />
<b>Siam Paragon</b><br />
Is one of the biggest shopping complex in Thailand, one can get lost in it, up to 4 floors but covering an area of 300,000 m2, has movie complexes in it as well as the most modern fashion clothes, electronics in cheap prices. The mall was very newly built which just opened on December 9th, 2005. There are also lots of Thai style goods and restaurants in it, tourists can buy goods there as gifts or memorial objects. <br />

				  <div id="views"><u><b>Highlights of Sampeng market:</b></u><br />
- Sampeng is one of the biggest and oldest market for fabrics and readymade clothing in Bangkok.<br />
- This soi has been closed for any 4-wheels vehicles and only motorcycles are allowed to enter.<br />
- This is the famous Sampeng Lane where you can buy cheap things.<br />
- The food market for you when you are tired from walking around.<br />
- In this walking tour, you also pass stalls selling dry food for example like mushrooms, nuts, fish etc.<br />
- Here is Bangkok's center of textile wholesalers that sell everything about clothing making such as buttons, zippers, laces, sewing equipment, gifts, souvenirs &amp; stationery and children &amp; teenager products.<br />
- The lane is only 2 meters wide and crowded with people, push carts and motorbikes.<br />
<br />
<u><b>Food:</b></u><br />
Local delicacies include Dim Sum, Teachew-style steamed Chinese mullet, shark fin in red sauce and various single noodle and rice dishes such as stir-fried noodles, casserole pork in noodles soup, rice with boiled chicken, rice with crispy pork or duck toppings, rice with curry and etc. Other appetizers and sweets are stuffed sheet noodles, pork sate, dumplings, bird's nest soup, soybean milk, lotus root juice and toasted chestnuts (locally called Kaolat).<br />
